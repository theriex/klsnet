Kim Hazeltine

Kim Hazeltine is an undergraduate student at the University of Massachusetts, Boston, majoring in psychology, and minoring in both English and Asian American studies. Her research interests reflect her experiences from a multiracial background as a mixed second generation Chinese American with paternal lineages tracing back to Ireland and Germany. She hopes to learn more about the intricacies of the psychological interstices of culture, race, ethnicity, and gender. Kim is a tutor for professor Tracey Dunne's Learning and Memory class and a member of the international psychology honors club, Psi Chi

Micaela Curley

Micaela Curley recently graduated with her Bachelors of Arts in Psychology from the University of Massachusetts, Boston. She completed a Psychology Honors thesis utilizing the AART team data on meanings of race and ethnicity, describing colloquial understandings of race and ethnicities, their functions, and the processes by which people define them. She is continuing to work on this project, preparing a publication to report her findings that will also explore differences related to demographic variables such as gender, immigrant generation, and self-identified race. Her research interests reflect her own experiences coming from a multiracial and multiethnic background, and include race, ethnicity, social class, and gender.
